# Sales-Performance-analysis-PowerBI
This report goes beyond raw numbers. Instead of just reporting what happened, it asks why it happened and what should be done about it. 

## Project Overview

This project is an end-to-end sales analysis built entirely in **Microsoft Excel 365**. It goes beyond building a dashboard — the goal was to extract **business meaning** from raw sales data, identify patterns, flag risks, and deliver strategic recommendations the way an analyst would in a real business context.

The dataset covers **5 months of sales data across 4 regions**, spanning multiple product categories including Technology and Accessories.

---

## Objectives

- Analyse revenue and profitability trends across time, region, and product
- Identify high-performing and underperforming products
- Uncover seasonal patterns and growth signals
- Translate raw data into actionable business recommendations

##  Tools & Techniques Used

| Tool / Feature | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Pivot Tables** | Summarising sales, profit, and margin by region/product/month |
| **Slicers** | Dynamic filtering on the dashboard |
| **XLOOKUP** | Automated data lookups across sheets |
| **INDEX-MATCH** | Flexible multi-criteria lookups |
| **KPI Cards** | Total Sales, Profit, Margin at a glance |
| **Charts & Trend Lines** | Monthly sales trend and growth rate visualisation |
| **Conditional Formatting** | Highlighting underperforming products (e.g. Laptop flagged in red) |

##  Key Findings

###  Revenue vs. Profitability
- Total Sales: **~₹25.3 Lakhs** | Total Profit: **~₹4.4 Lakhs** | Margin: **~17%**
- Profitable but not highly efficient — clear scope for margin improvement

###  Regional Performance
- **East region** leads in sales (~₹6.5L), but all regions share nearly identical margins (0.17–0.18)
- Uniform margins signal a standardized pricing strategy with no regional optimization

###  Product Insights
- **Phone** is the clear revenue and profit flagship
- **Laptop** underperforms — lower sales and weaker margins
- **Technology (~70%)** dominates revenue, creating category dependency risk

###  Trend Analysis
- Sales show a **gradual declining trend** with growth rate of only **+2.23%**
- **October** = best month (festive season); **July** = weakest month (off-season)
- Business is stable but not scaling — early warning signal

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Improve margins** — review pricing strategy and reduce operational costs
2. **Fix the product portfolio** — reposition or bundle underperforming products like Laptop
3. **Unlock regional potential** — introduce dynamic, region-specific pricing
4. **Build a seasonal sales plan** — campaigns in July, scale up for October
5. **Reduce category dependency** — grow accessories portfolio, introduce cross-selling
